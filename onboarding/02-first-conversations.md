# 02 — First Conversations

## Objective

Your first conversations should establish operating quality immediately.
They should be specific, constraint-aware, and structured for iteration.

Below are advanced examples for personal performance domains using the same rigor expected in professional work.

## Example 1: fitness training plan under real constraints

**User**

> I want a 6-week training plan focused on strength maintenance and moderate conditioning improvement.
>
> Constraints:
> - 4 sessions/week max
> - 60 minutes/session on weekdays
> - travel during weeks 3 and 6 with hotel-gym limitations
> - protect sleep and avoid recovery debt during heavy work cycles
>
> Output format:
> 1) weekly structure
> 2) session templates
> 3) progression logic
> 4) fatigue-risk flags
> 5) adaptation rules for missed sessions
>
> Challenge my assumptions if the goal-load-constraint mix is inconsistent.

**Why this works**

- Defines objective and constraints clearly.
- Requests structure, not generic tips.
- Explicitly asks for pushback and risk control.

## Example 2: nutrition decision support for consistency, not novelty

**User**

> I need a weekday nutrition framework that improves energy stability and body-composition control without high complexity.
>
> Context:
> - already follow clean eating standards
> - frequent early meetings and occasional late dinners
> - high cognitive load during market and deal pressure weeks
>
> Build a practical system with:
> - default meal architecture
> - contingency options for disrupted days
> - trade-offs between strictness and adherence
> - measurable weekly indicators to track whether the plan is working
>
> Do not provide motivational guidance. Prioritize repeatability and decision simplicity.

**Why this works**

- Anchors to operational objective (stability + adherence).
- Forces trade-off analysis.
- Emphasizes measurable validation.

## Example 3: golf improvement plan as a constrained skill system

**User**

> Build a 10-week golf improvement framework focused on approach-play dispersion and scoring consistency.
>
> Constraints:
> - two range sessions/week
> - one weekend round
> - total weekly practice time <= 4 hours
>
> Output:
> 1) weekly practice allocation
> 2) drill sequencing rationale
> 3) performance metrics (leading and lagging)
> 4) monthly review protocol
> 5) what to deprioritize to avoid diluted practice
>
> Add a red-team section: where this plan is likely to fail and how to mitigate.

**Why this works**

- Treats golf as a measurable performance domain.
- Requires explicit prioritization and anti-bloat discipline.
- Includes adversarial review before execution.

## Execution standard

If the first conversation does not produce a plan with explicit constraints, trade-offs, metrics, and failure modes, the briefing was not specific enough.
