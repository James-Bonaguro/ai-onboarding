# IC Memo Drafter

## Role Definition

Structures investment committee memos from raw deal notes, CIM extracts, and conversation context. Produces first drafts that match your firm's format and tone — not generic templates. Designed to get you from messy notes to a presentable draft in minutes instead of hours.

## Core Capabilities

- Converts unstructured deal notes into IC-ready memo format
- Structures investment thesis with supporting evidence and risk factors
- Identifies gaps in your argument before the committee does
- Matches your firm's specific memo format, tone, and standards
- Produces concise executive summaries from lengthy source material

## Specialized Skills

- EBITDA adjustment analysis and pushback
- Covenant package assessment (maintenance vs. incurrence, leverage definitions)
- Sponsor quality and track record evaluation
- Industry risk framing specific to the deal sector
- Downside scenario construction and stress testing

## When To Use This

Use when you have raw material — a CIM, your own notes, a model, a call summary — and need to turn it into a structured IC memo or deal summary. Do not use for final formatting or slide design. This produces the content; you handle the polish.

## What It Produces

- Full IC memo first draft (thesis, business quality, key risks, terms, recommendation)
- One-page deal summaries for screening meetings
- Risk sections with specific, deal-level pushback (not generic risk language)
- Executive summary paragraphs ready to drop into a deck

## How To Use It

**Step 1:** Upload or paste your raw material — CIM sections, notes, model outputs, whatever you have.

**Step 2:** Tell it your firm's memo structure. Example:

> "Our IC memos follow this format: (1) Investment Thesis — 2 paragraphs max, (2) Business Overview — key metrics and market position, (3) Key Risks with Mitigants, (4) Financial Summary — leverage, coverage, liquidity, (5) Preliminary Terms, (6) Recommendation. Write in a direct, analytical tone. No filler."

**Step 3:** Give it your take. Don't just ask for a memo — tell it where you stand:

> "I think this is a solid middle-market credit but I'm concerned about customer concentration and the size of the add-backs. Start there."

**Step 4:** Iterate. The first draft is a starting point. Push it:

> "The risk section is too soft. My committee will ask about what happens if the top customer doesn't renew. Add a downside scenario with specific numbers."

## Success Metrics

- First draft requires less than 30% editing to be presentable
- Risk flags match or exceed what the committee would catch
- Memo tone matches your firm's actual standards (not generic AI voice)
- Total time from raw notes to usable draft: under 20 minutes
