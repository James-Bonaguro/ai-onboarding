# Start here

*A note for the two people this was built for.*

I made you something. It's a website — one link, no apps to install, nothing to
sign up for. It teaches you how to actually use AI for your job (the real
stuff: CIMs, credit memos, covenants, models, IC and LP work), not the generic
"write me a poem" nonsense.

You don't need to know anything technical. If you ever see a word that looks like
developer jargon, ignore it — it's not for you.

## What to click

👉 **[https://james-bonaguro.github.io/ai-onboarding/](https://james-bonaguro.github.io/ai-onboarding/)**

Open it on your laptop (it works on your phone too). It looks good in light or
dark — there's a little sun/moon button in the top-right if you want to switch.

## What to do first — give it 30 minutes

1. **Skim "The Rules"** (about 8 minutes). Five short ideas that change how AI
   behaves for you. Don't overthink it — you can come back.
2. **Do "Setup"** (about 12 minutes). You'll copy two paragraphs I already wrote
   for you and paste them into your AI's settings. That's the whole setup. After
   this, every answer it gives you is sharper and sounds like it knows your job.
   Use **Copilot** for anything with real deal information in it — it's the one
   your firm cleared. Use ChatGPT or Claude for practice and learning.
3. **Run one real prompt** (about 10 minutes). Go to "Prompts," hit **Copy** on
   *"Summarize a CIM for credit review,"* paste it into Copilot, attach a CIM
   you're actually working on, and read what comes back. That's your first win.

That's it. If you only do those three things today, you're ahead of most of your
firm already.

## Then what

Once you've felt it work, do **one drill a day** from "The First 30 Days" —
fifteen minutes each, at your desk, on deals you're already touching. There's a
progress bar that remembers where you are. Six weeks of that and you'll be
noticeably faster and sharper than the people next to you. The "Workflows" and
"The Play" sections are there when you want them.

Text me when you've done the first 30 minutes. I want to hear what the CIM
summary spat out.
