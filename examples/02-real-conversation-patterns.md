# 02 — Real Conversation Patterns

## Objective

Conversation patterns that work in underwriting and memo work also work in personal performance domains.
The domain changes; the operating model does not.

## Reusable pattern

1. **Brief clearly** — objective, constraints, current state.
2. **Request structure** — decision-ready format, not freeform advice.
3. **Force trade-offs** — what to optimize, what to sacrifice.
4. **Require pushback** — surface weak assumptions and likely failure points.
5. **Iterate deliberately** — refine based on execution feedback.

## Fitness example (pattern in practice)

**Brief**

> Design a training week that preserves strength while reducing fatigue volatility during high-intensity work periods. 4 sessions max, 60-minute cap, and one travel day.

**Structure request**

> Provide: weekly split, session templates, progression rules, and fatigue escalation triggers.

**Trade-off request**

> Show two versions: performance-biased vs recovery-biased. Explain when each is superior.

**Pushback request**

> Identify where my workload assumptions are unrealistic and what to cut first.

**Iteration prompt**

> Week 1 feedback: lower-body day exceeded time cap and sleep dropped. Rebuild week 2 while preserving core objective.

## Nutrition example (pattern in practice)

**Brief**

> Build a weekday nutrition operating system for energy stability and composition control with minimal decision overhead.

**Structure request**

> Give meal defaults, contingency rules for disrupted days, and a weekly review checklist.

**Trade-off request**

> Compare strict macro precision vs rule-based portions for my schedule. Recommend one and defend it.

**Pushback request**

> Challenge any part of my current approach that likely reduces adherence over 90 days.

**Iteration prompt**

> I missed two evening meals due to late calls. Redesign contingency logic without adding prep complexity.

## Golf example (pattern in practice)

**Brief**

> Build a constrained practice system to improve approach dispersion from 120-170 yards within a 4-hour weekly cap.

**Structure request**

> Include practice blocks, drill order, scoring protocol, and monthly review format.

**Trade-off request**

> Compare “volume of reps” vs “narrowed high-quality reps” for this objective.

**Pushback request**

> Identify where I may be overemphasizing mechanics and underweighting scoring transfer.

**Iteration prompt**

> Month 1 result: range contact improved, scoring did not. Redesign the next cycle to increase on-course transfer.

## Bottom line

Do not switch into a softer standard for personal domains.
Use the same briefing discipline, challenge standards, and iteration logic you use at work.
