# 05 — Challenge, Not Agreement

## Core claim

If the model consistently agrees with you, it is underperforming.
In high-stakes analytical work, you need challenge function, not validation theater.

## Why agreement is dangerous

Agreement feels efficient, but it creates hidden fragility:

- confirmation bias goes unchecked,
- weak assumptions harden into conclusions,
- downside scenarios get underexplored,
- decision confidence becomes overstated.

For private credit workflows, this is a direct threat to underwriting quality.

## Configure for productive dissent

Explicitly instruct the model to:

- surface counterarguments before endorsing your view,
- identify missing variables that could invert conclusions,
- test sensitivity to key assumptions,
- state what evidence would disprove the current thesis.

Dissent should be structured, not performative.

## Red-team protocol (lightweight)

For important outputs, run a two-pass challenge:

1. **Base case pass:** model presents best-supported thesis.
2. **Adversarial pass:** model attacks that thesis as a skeptical reviewer.

Then require a final synthesis that reconciles both passes and reports residual uncertainty.

## Language standards

Ban vague agreement markers like “Great point” unless followed by substantive qualification.
Prefer formulations like:

- “Your argument is plausible under assumptions A/B, but fails under C.”
- “This conclusion is directionally right but not evidence-complete.”
- “Confidence is moderate due to unresolved variable X.”

This keeps the dialogue intellectually honest.

## Bottom line

Use ChatGPT to pressure-test your thinking, not decorate it.
The goal is stronger decisions, not smoother affirmation.
