# Rule 6: How You Actually Get Good at This

## The Honest Answer

There's no course, no certification, no YouTube playlist that teaches this. The people who are best at using AI all have the same story: they just started using it.

Not by studying it. By doing things with it.

## It's a Learning Accelerator

The gap between "I have ChatGPT" and "I'm producing better work twice as fast" isn't knowledge. It's reps. You need to build the instinct for how to give it context, how to steer it, and when to push back.

The fastest way to build that instinct is to start using AI as a learning accelerator in your day-to-day life. Not to replace your thinking — to expand it. Use it to figure out things you wouldn't have figured out on your own. Use it to learn things faster than you could have learned them before.

**Get in the habit:**
- Planning a trip? Pull up AI and plan it together. You'll learn more about where you're going and get a better itinerary than you would browsing ten websites.
- Comparing two products before buying? Ask AI to break down the tradeoffs. You'll make a sharper decision and train yourself to ask better questions.
- Something wrong with your car? Describe the symptoms and get a real explanation of what's likely happening before you walk into the shop.
- Trying to understand how something works — a financial concept, a tax rule, a negotiation tactic? Use it to go deeper than a Google search ever could.

Every one of these builds the same muscles you'll use at work: giving context, being specific, iterating, pushing back. And you learn something real in the process.

**Challenge yourself.** How many times a day do you run into something you're curious about, or something you don't fully understand, and just move on? Start pulling out AI in those moments instead. Make it a habit. That's the practice.

## The Voice Trick

This is the single most underrated way to use AI, and almost nobody talks about it.

**Put your headphones in. Go for a walk. Talk.**

Most AI tools have a voice mode — or you can just use your phone's voice-to-text and paste it in. When you talk out loud, you think differently than when you type. You're less filtered. You say what you actually mean instead of trying to write the "perfect prompt."

Some of the best uses:
- You're stuck on a problem at work. Walk for 15 minutes and just explain the problem out loud to the AI. Half the time, you'll solve it yourself while talking. The other half, the AI catches something you missed.
- You have a vague idea but can't articulate it. Talk it through. Then say: "Okay, I just rambled for 5 minutes. Organize what I said into a clear structure."
- You need to draft something but you're staring at a blank screen. Talk through what you want to say. Then ask the AI to clean it up.

Voice-to-text doesn't produce clean, structured input. That's actually the point. Raw thinking goes in, structured output comes out. The AI is very good at that translation.

## What Getting Good Actually Looks Like

**Week 1:** You use it for basic questions and the answers feel generic. You think it's overhyped.

**Week 3:** You start giving it more context and the answers get noticeably better. You catch yourself thinking "I should ask the AI about this" throughout the day.

**Month 2:** You stop thinking about "prompts" entirely. You just talk to it. You get annoyed when the output isn't sharp enough, and you know exactly what to say to fix it.

**Month 4:** Colleagues notice your work is faster and better. They ask what you're doing differently. You can't fully explain it because it's become instinct.

**That progression doesn't come from reading about AI. It comes from making it part of how you think and learn every day.**

## The One-Sentence Rule

**The best way to get good at AI is to use it as a learning accelerator for things you actually care about — not to study it for something you might need later.**
