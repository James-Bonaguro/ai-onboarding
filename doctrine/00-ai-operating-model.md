# AI Operating Model

Most people approach AI incorrectly.

They treat it like search.

Example:

"Explain private credit."  
"Summarize this article."  
"What is EBITDA?"

This produces shallow results.

The real value appears when AI is treated as a **collaborative reasoning system**.

Instead of asking isolated questions, the user provides structured context and works through problems iteratively.

---

# The Core Interaction Model

Effective AI usage follows a simple structure.

Context -> Objective -> Constraints -> Iteration

---

## 1. Context

AI performs best when it understands the environment surrounding a problem.

Examples of useful context:

- industry
- role
- decision objective
- assumptions
- relevant data
- time horizon

Without context, responses default to generic explanations.

Context is the most important input variable.

---

## 2. Objective

The user should clearly define the task.

Examples:

- evaluate a company
- pressure test an argument
- structure research
- generate alternative explanations
- summarize complex information

Vague objectives produce vague output.

---

## 3. Constraints

Constraints shape the quality of the response.

Examples:

- desired output format
- time horizon
- risk tolerance
- decision criteria
- information limits

Constraints make analysis sharper and more useful.

---

## 4. Iteration

The first response is rarely the best one.

High-skill AI users refine output through iteration.

Examples:

- ask for stronger reasoning
- request counterarguments
- clarify assumptions
- restructure analysis
- add missing context

The skill is not prompting.

The skill is **steering the conversation toward clarity**.

---

# The Role of Challenge

AI should not simply agree with the user.

A useful analytical partner should:

- identify weak assumptions
- highlight missing information
- propose alternative interpretations
- surface risks or counterpoints

This makes AI valuable as a reasoning partner rather than a passive assistant.

---

# The Goal

The goal is not to ask better questions.

The goal is to **integrate AI into the process of thinking, analysis, and decision-making**.
