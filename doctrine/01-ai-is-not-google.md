# 01 — AI Is Not Google

## Core claim

Treating ChatGPT like a search engine guarantees mediocre outcomes.
Search retrieves documents.
ChatGPT constructs responses.
That distinction changes everything about how you should use it.

## What goes wrong with the “Google mindset” 

When users ask short, ambiguous questions and expect one-shot certainty, three failure modes appear:

1. **Shallow relevance:** you get plausible generalities, not decision-grade analysis.
2. **Hidden assumptions:** the model fills missing context with defaults you did not choose.
3. **False confidence:** fluent writing can mask weak logic or missing evidence.

In private credit, these are not cosmetic issues; they are risk issues.

## Correct operating model

Use ChatGPT as a **structured reasoning collaborator**, not a retrieval endpoint.

That means:

- define objective and decision context before asking for output,
- specify constraints, evaluation criteria, and output format,
- force explicit assumptions and confidence levels,
- request challenges to your base case.

## Practical translation

Instead of: “Summarize this company quickly.”

Use: “Assess this borrower for downside resilience over a 24-month horizon. Separate facts, assumptions, and inferences. Identify top 5 covenant pressure points, assign confidence levels, and list what incremental data would most change the view.”

The second request yields a usable work product because it frames a decision problem.

## Standard to enforce

If a response cannot answer:

- What is known?
- What is assumed?
- What is inferred?
- What could break this conclusion?

then you are still in “Google mode,” not analytical mode.

## Bottom line

The leverage of ChatGPT is not in finding answers faster.
It is in structuring thought under uncertainty.
Use it accordingly.
