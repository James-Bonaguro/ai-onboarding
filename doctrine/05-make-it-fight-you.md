# Rule 5: Make It Fight You

## The Default Problem

By default, AI agrees with everything you say.

You say: "I think this is a strong credit." It says: "Great analysis! Here are some additional reasons this is a strong credit."

That's not helpful. That's dangerous. You just got your own thesis repeated back to you with a bow on it.

## What To Do Instead

Tell it to push back. Explicitly. Every time it matters.

**Instead of:**
> "What do you think of my investment thesis?"

**Say:**
> "Here's my investment thesis. I want you to tear it apart. Find the three weakest points in my logic. Don't worry about being polite — I need to know where this breaks before my credit committee does."

## Why This Matters

In private credit, the whole game is downside protection. You already know the bull case — the sponsor told you the bull case. What you need is someone to find the holes before your IC does.

AI can do this extremely well, but only if you tell it to. Otherwise it defaults to being an agreeable assistant that confirms your existing beliefs.

## The Setup

Add this to your custom instructions (the ones from Rule 2):

> "When I share analysis or a thesis, your job is to find the weaknesses — not to validate me. If my logic has gaps, say so directly. If my assumptions are aggressive, flag them. I'd rather hear the problems from you now than from my committee later."

Once this is in your settings, it runs automatically. You don't have to ask for pushback every time — it just does it.

## Real Example

**Without the setting:**
> You: "I think this company's 20% EBITDA margins are sustainable."
> AI: "That's a reasonable view. The company has demonstrated consistent margins over the past three years."

**With the setting:**
> You: "I think this company's 20% EBITDA margins are sustainable."
> AI: "Those margins are currently supported by a single long-term contract that reprices in 18 months. If you lose even 200bps on the renewal, your debt service coverage drops below 1.2x. Are you sizing the facility to the current margin or to a downside scenario?"

Same input from you. Completely different value in the response.

## The One-Sentence Rule

**The most valuable thing your AI can do is tell you where you're wrong before someone else does.**
